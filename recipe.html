Egg Curry
  1. 4 Whole Eggs hard boiled
  2. 6 Tomatoes cooked and pureed
  3. 1 teaspoon Turmeric powder (Haldi)
  4. 1 teaspoon Garam masala powder
  5. 1 teaspoon Coriander Powder (Dhania)
  6. 1/2 teaspoon Red Chilli powder
  7. 1/2 teaspoon Sugar, Salt to taste
  8. Coriander (Dhania) Leaves
  9. few leaves, finely chopped
  10. Oil for cooking
  1. To begin making the Quick & Easy Egg Curry Recipe, Firstly we will boil the eggs. Add the eggs in a saucepan with some water. Add sufficient water that the eggs are covered with it. Let it come to boil for 15 minutes or untill you see a crack on the outer shell.
  2. Once done, let it cool down. Remove the outer shell of the egg and keep it aside.
  3. Heat 2 tablespoons of oil in a wok; add in the onion garlic and ginger paste. Sauté for a few minutes until the raw smell goes away and the onion changes color.
  4. Once the onion is well roasted, add in the turmeric powder, sugar, garam masala powder, coriander power and red chilli powder. Stir those powders into the onion masala until well combined. This will take about 3 to 4 minutes.
  5. After 3 to 4 minutes, add in the tomato puree, salt and the eggs. Simmer the Egg Curry covered for 10 to 15 minutes until you see the eggs are coated well with the curry.
  6. Check the salt and spice levels and the consistency of the curry. Adjust them to taste and add water if you want the consistency of the curry to be thinner. Once done, stir in the chopped coriander leaves and serve.
  7. Quick And Simple Egg Curry Recipe makes a delicious dinner or a Sunday brunch when served along with Steamed Rice, Carrot Salad and a Boondi Raita.
Chicken Biryani
  1. Chicken
  2. Rice
  3. Ginger and Garlic paste
  4. Fresh herbs
  5. Onions
  6. Bay leaves, whole black cardamom, star anise, cinnamon stick, cloves, peppercorns, and Shahi Jeera.
  7. Spices and seasonings
  8. Milk + Saffron strands
  9. Yogurt + lime juice
  10. Ghee + oil
  1. Clean and wash the chicken pieces thoroughly under tap water. Drain the water completely. Transfer chicken to a large mixing bowl and add lemon juice. Rub the juice all over the chicken. Add ginger and garlic paste.
  2. You can add fresh cilantro leaves and mint leaves to the marinade too. Add yogurt to the spice mix. Mix and roll the chicken to coat in the marinade. Chicken Biryani Cover and allow it to rest for an hour.
  3. Heat oil in a pan. Dust of the excess cornstarch and add a small batch of onion rings to the hot oil. When the oil is hot, add the onions. Never add the onions to cold oil; they will not become crispy.
  4. Let’s move on to the next step. Add milk to a microwave-safe bowl. Heat the milk for 40 seconds. Remove from the microwave and add saffron strands to the milk. Stir until combined and set aside.
  5. Once the rice is parboiled, turn the heat off. Remove the rice in the strainer.
  6. Add sliced onions to the spices. Cook until the onions are lightly golden in color.
  7. Spread it evenly all over the chicken. The chicken should be covered with a generous layer of rice.
  8. Generously add fried onions or birista all over the biryani
  9. Turn the heat on and cook the biryani for 2-3 minutes on high heat. After that, lower the heat to the lowest setting and let the biryani simmer for 10-12 minutes.
  10. It’s time to lift the lid. Gently fluff the rice with a spatula. Hidden underneath the rice, you will discover tender, juicy pieces of chicken bursting with fantastic flavor from all the spices and herbs.
POTATO CURRY
  1. 1½ kg (3lbs) potatoes
  2. 1 onion finely chopped
  3. 4 garlic cloves minced, 2 tsp ginger
  4. 1 tbsp garam masala, ½ tsp ground cardamom
  5. 1 tsp chilli powder, 1 tsp ground turmeric
  6. 400 g (14oz) chopped tomatoes, 1 tsp sugar
  7. 3 cups stock/broth, salt and pepper to taste
  1. Peel the potatoes and chop into bite-size chunks. .
  2. Place the potatoes in a large pot, top with water and season with salt. Bring the water up to a boil and parboil for 10 minutes.
  3. Drain and set aside. Saute the onions, garlic and ginger in a large pot until soft and fragrant.
  4. Add the spices and cook for another minute. Add the tomatoes and sugar then cook for 2 minutes. Add the potatoes and stir to coat in the sauce.
  5. Pour in the stock then season to taste. Reduce the heat and simmer for 25-30 minutes or until the potatoes are cooked through and the sauce has thickened.
  6. Serve the curry with roti, yogurt and chopped chillies
Sambar Curry
  1. Tuvar Dal
  2. Vegetables
  3. Tamarind Paste
  4. Sambar Powder
  5. Oil
  6. turmeric powder, salt, curry leaves, whole dry red chilies
  7. mustard seeds, onions, and tomatoes
  1. Wash dal several times in running water.Soak the dal for at least 10 minutes. In a pressure cooker add soaked toor dal, 2 cups water, garlic cloves and castor oil.
  2. Pressure cook on high flame for 3 whistles and simmer for 10 mins. Let the pressure release naturally. Meanwhile in a pan heat oil, add mustard seeds.
  3. Once the mustard seeds crackle, add chana dal, urad dal and cumin seeds. Add asafoetida. Once the dals are roasted, add chopped shallots, red chillies, green chillies and curry leaves. Once the shallots turn translucent, add chopped tomatoes.
  4. Saute till the tomatoes turn mushy. Now add sambar powder, turmeric powder, mix well and switch off the flame.
  5. In the same pressure cooker, mash the dal slightly, add the chopped vegetables and 1 ½ cups of water and bring it to a boil. You can adjust the quantity of water as per your need. This yields a thick sambar. If you need a slightly runny one add half a cup of extra water. Now add the tempered mixture and mix well.
  6. Add tamarind water or tamarind paste. Add required salt.
  7. Pressure cook on high flame for just 1 whistle and release the pressure manually since we have used drumsticks. If you are not using drumsticks you can allow the pressure to release naturally. Garnish with coriander leaves.
  8. Serve hot!!!
Paneer Curry
  1. ½ tsp Orient Asian Red Chilli Paste
  2. 1 tsp oil 4 Cloves 4 Green cardamoms
  3. 1 inch piece Cinnamon 1 Onion (sauteed till golden brown and blended with little water to make a paste) Paste of 1 tsp ginger and 1 tsp garlic with 4 green chillies
  4. 3 tbsp Dahi / Yogurt 1 tsp Dhaniya / Coriander powder 1 tsp Pepper powder
  5. 3/4 tsp Cumin powder 1 tsp Salt 2 tbsp Cream
  6. 250 gram Paneer To garnish Coriander To garnish Fresh mint leaves
  1. Heat oil and include the cloves, cardamoms and cinnamon till it’s overall quite fragrant. Presently include onion paste and cook well.
  2. Include ginger, garlic and green chilli paste. Saute. Include yoghurt.
  3. Cook and include coriander powder, cumin powder, pepper powder, Orient Asian Red Chilli Paste. Include salt. Cook and include paneer and cream and 1/2 cup water.
  4. Seal the dish with foil and put a cover on top. Decrease the heat to low and let it stew for 15 minutes.
  5. Presently open it and cook further on the off chance that you like to dry it. Check for salt including more if necessary, serve garnished with coriander and mint leaves.
